Research

My research sits at the intersection of natural language processing, machine learning, and real-world decision making, with a strong focus on African contexts and responsible AI.

NLP for African & Low-Resource Languages

Building datasets, benchmark tasks, and models for under-resourced African languages, focusing on sentiment analysis, machine translation, code-switching, and culturally grounded language understanding.

Multimodal & Multilingual Learning

Designing multimodal pipelines that combine text, images, and signals for applications such as sign-language translation, digital forensics, and ESG reporting, with an emphasis on robustness and interpretability.

Digital Forensic Readiness & Authorship

Integrating conventional machine learning with deep learning to detect and characterise cybercriminal activities, support authorship attribution, and enhance digital forensic readiness through automated alerts.

AI for Finance, Governance & Sustainability

Applying AI to ESG and sustainability disclosures, risk analysis, and financial narratives, aligning technical innovation with social impact, ethics, and regulatory frameworks.

Selected Projects

A sample of applied and research projects at the interface of AI, language, and society. Many are implemented as open-source or reproducible pipelines.

Authorship & Forensic Linguistics

Developing neural authorship attribution models and stylistic embeddings to assist in forensic investigations and integrity checking of digital texts.

NLP for African Languages

Building datasets and models for Yoruba and other African languages, including idiom understanding, sentiment analysis, and code-switched text modelling with reinforcement learning and retrieval-augmented generation.

AI for ESG & Sustainability Reporting

Analysing sustainability and governance disclosures with AI to support transparency, accountability, and alignment with SDGs and governance codes.
